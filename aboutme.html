Overview

Family

I was born to a typical middle class family in Central Kerala to an engineer and a banker mom.My dad used to work abroad, but is now enjoying his late 50s at his own abode.

I am still confused whether this a boon or bane, I have a brother. Having a brother almost 10 years older than you means that he can be bossy like your parents but can also be kind enough to sign your report cards(Yeah!the not so good ones) or to get yourself some good pocket money or to be your lifeline or teacher on the day before exams.

Most part of my childhood took place in the company of seven cousins who used to live close-by ensuring that boredom was not in my dictionary.(Well,the latest edition do have that word. Education and job spread us apart). My childhood was made awesome by my grandparents who turned out to be the reserve bank governer for my little wants and a masterchef candidate whenever my stomach pleased.

School

I spent the first two years of my schooling in Sree narayana Vidyapeetham.And I dont remember even a tiniest bit about my life there

I joined Chinmaya Vidyalaya Tripunithura once i got promoted to first grade. Life got exciting during the 12 years at that place. I made a lot of friends and we grew up together with our own little problems and ways to sort them out.

As a group of naughty students and loving teachers, we found ourselves exploring horizons together and having a lot of fun in doing so.

I was frightened on the first day of school when my mother turned her back on me and walked away, leaving me behind with a bunch of kids. On that day I never knew that i would be once sad when I leave those kids behind me.Just as all good things come to an end I graduated from high school in 2019

College

What i would do after school was always a question. I had interest in none of the things that undergraduation had to offer me. just like anyone with similar circumstances and un-hate(I would never use the word love here) for PCM, engineering was the default option.

The hard part was that my family expected me to study hard for Jee.Fun fact: I never intended to.. And that was when i chance to stumble upon the aftermovie of Oasis(the annual cultural fest of BITSpilani). Yes! that is the place.I went onto watch a lot of Aftermovies, but none of them gave them the vibe Oasis gave(Not even IITB).

Life was just another Daku-Mangal-Singh and i was no Chotta Bheem to fight what he has in store for me. I failed in JEE(expected and safe) and even more miserably in BITSAT(heartbroken). I made it to almost all the top colleges in Kerala. But having a brother whose life turned out to be well from a private University based in Coimbatore and a family who have a not so good approach towards KTU, I knew what exactly was coming for me

I was trailing on my brothers footpaths and I joined Amrita University for Computer Science. Well, life today is not so bad at this new place. i have started making friends, and coping up with life away from home. If good things keep on happening, I am sure that life @ amrita is gonna be one hell of a fun and great ride
